A great corner. The wrong tenant just collapsed. Let's put the right one in.

A clean, direct lease with an experienced management team and a licensed operating partner. Fresh deposit, fresh guaranty, reopened in weeks — and the prior tenant's arrears stay where they belong.

Today

Two glowing storefronts. One dark middle door.

The block is alive on either side. In the middle sits a licensed dispensary that reads as closed — no awning, no glow, no reason for anyone walking past to slow down. It feels like the block forgot it was there.

After relaunch

Three reasons to stop on the corner.

Illuminated awning. A line at the door. The Q18 pulling in. The block finally working as one destination instead of two with a dead spot between them.

The current tenant operating the dispensary at 30-12 Astoria Blvd — Dominica 62 — is in full collapse and will not continue as a functioning business. You're moving forward with eviction. That leaves the familiar bad set of options: a dark licensed dispensary, months of vacancy, and arrears you'll be chasing against an entity that no longer operates. Here's the cleaner path: rather than re-tenant a distressed, dark box on the open market, execute a new, direct lease with our management company. We bring the capital, the operating infrastructure, and a qualified licensed dispensary partner — then rebrand and reopen fast. A new lease, a capitalized tenant of record, an experienced team, and a license partner in good standing. The arrears Dominica 62 left behind stay with Dominica 62 — yours to pursue through the eviction. We're not asking you to discount them. We're offering a materially stronger tenant than the one you're losing.

How this works

Clean lease. Reopened in weeks. Built out from there.

No forward-dating, no flip, no contingency on the prior tenant. A new, direct lease signed now — then we move fast: rebrand and reopen, with systems live on day one, and a focused capital build-out after.

01
At signing · The clean lease

A new, direct lease — and the keys.

  • A new, direct lease between you and our management company at market terms. We are the tenant of record and the party on the hook to you.
  • Fresh security deposit posted at signing.
  • Personal guaranty from the management principals — a real, capitalized guaranty, not the one walking out the door.
  • Tax returns & full financial information on every principal — delivered under NDA before signing, within days.
  • A qualified, licensed dispensary partner holds the OCM license and operates from the premises under a management & services agreement.
02
Within ~5 days · Rebrand & reopen

Day 5: it looks like the renderings.

  • Visible facelift complete within five days of taking the keys — exterior signage, awning, window graphics, paint, lighting, shelving, finishes. The space matches the renderings already on this site.
  • Marketing program deployed. Local print, bus-shelter QR, social, brand identity, grand-reopening campaign. The larger early budget line; visibility is the binding constraint, not the space.
  • Systems live. POS, payments and cashless processing, inventory, compliance, and banking — all stood up at reopening.
03
Months 1–6 · Strategic build-out

Targeted capital. Throughput, not renovation.

  • Storage area build-out. Proper shelving, security, and intake/check-in workflow. The current layout is a constraint on inventory handling and turnover.
  • Throughput improvements. Checkout flow, fast-pickup lane, cashless processing — targeted investment where customer flow is currently slowed.
  • The premise: the location does not need a wholesale rebuild. It needs the right tenant, basic finish work, a serious marketing push, and focused capital in the places that move the business.
